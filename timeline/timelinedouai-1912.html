Douai Mid-Air Collision – Detailed Timeline

🛫 Aerial Demonstration – June 19, 1912
Afternoon – June 19, 1912

Military flying demonstration begins at the Douai airbase in northern France with multiple aircraft in formation.

~14:35 – June 19, 1912

Two monoplane aircraft — operated by French Army pilots — are seen performing parallel maneuvers at low altitude.

⚠️ Collision – June 19, 1912
~14:37 – June 19, 1912

The aircraft unexpectedly converge during a turn and collide mid-air. One wing detaches completely from the leading aircraft.

Moments later – June 19, 1912

Both aircraft spiral downward and crash to the ground within 200 meters of each other.

💥 Casualties & Aftermath – June 19, 1912
Immediately – June 19, 1912

Onlookers rush to the scene. Both pilots are declared dead at impact.

Following day – June 20, 1912

French Army initiates internal review and suspends future demonstration flights pending investigation.
