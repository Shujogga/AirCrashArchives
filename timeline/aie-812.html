Air India Express Flight 812 – Mangalore Crash

Date of Incident: May 22, 2010

Location: Mangalore International Airport, India

🧭 What Happened

Flight IX-812 was a routine international service from Dubai to Mangalore. Upon landing, the Boeing 737-800 overshot the tabletop runway and plunged into a ravine, bursting into flames. Of the 166 onboard, 158 perished. It remains one of India’s deadliest aviation disasters.

✈️ Aircraft Details

Flight Number: IX-812

Aircraft Type: Boeing 737-8HG (SFP)

Registration: VT-AXV

Departure: Dubai International Airport (DXB)

Destination: Mangalore International Airport (IXE)

Fatalities: 158 (152 passengers + 6 crew)

Survivors: 8

🔍 Key Factors

The captain continued an unstabilized approach despite three calls from the first officer to go around. The aircraft touched down over halfway down the runway, leaving insufficient distance to stop. A late attempt to initiate a go-around failed, and the aircraft crashed into a ravine.

📆 Timeline of Events

  • 21:06 UTC – Flight departs Dubai
  • 00:35 UTC – Touchdown at Mangalore (5,200 ft down runway)
  • 00:36 UTC – Aircraft overruns and crashes into ravine
  • 00:40 UTC – Fire breaks out; rescue begins
  • 01:00 UTC – Emergency services reach site

🎙️ Cockpit Voice & Flight Data

“Go around, Captain.” — First Officer (transmitted on ATC frequency)
“We don’t have runway left!” — Final CVR entry

The CVR revealed the captain was asleep for much of the cruise. Despite multiple warnings and calls, the approach was continued. The CVR and FDR were recovered and analyzed by Indian authorities.

⚙️ Aircraft Systems & Failures

  • Unstabilized approach with excessive altitude and speed
  • Touchdown occurred 5,200 ft into an 8,033 ft runway
  • Late go-around initiated after thrust reversers deployed
  • Right wing struck ILS antenna; aircraft fell into ravine

🛡️ Aftermath and Reforms

  • DGCA revised pilot training for critical airfields
  • Runway safety audits intensified across India
  • Memorial erected near crash site (later vandalized)
  • Air India Express faced scrutiny over crew fatigue policies
