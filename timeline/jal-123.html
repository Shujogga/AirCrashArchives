Japan Airlines Flight 123 – Detailed Timeline

🛫 Departure & Initial Failure – August 12, 1985
18:12 JST – August 12, 1985

Flight 123 departs Tokyo Haneda Airport bound for Osaka with 524 people aboard a Boeing 747SR-46 (JA8119).

18:24 JST – August 12, 1985

Explosive decompression occurs at 24,000 ft over Sagami Bay. Vertical stabilizer detaches; all hydraulic systems fail.

18:25–18:30 JST – August 12, 1985

Captain declares emergency and requests return to Haneda. Aircraft begins uncontrollable phugoid oscillations.

✈️ Loss of Control & Descent – August 12, 1985
18:33 JST – August 12, 1985

Flight enters Dutch roll and erratic altitude changes. Crew attempts to stabilize using engine thrust alone.

18:40–18:47 JST – August 12, 1985

Aircraft circles over Gunma Prefecture. ATC offers diversion to Nagoya or Yokota; crew insists on Haneda.

18:50 JST – August 12, 1985

Flight descends to 7,000 ft, then climbs again to 13,000 ft. Crew struggles to maintain control.

💥 Impact & Rescue – August 12–13, 1985
18:56 JST – August 12, 1985

Aircraft crashes into Mount Takamagahara near Ueno Village, Gunma. Massive explosion and fire ensue.

Morning – August 13, 1985

Rescue teams reach crash site after delays. Only 4 survivors found; 520 fatalities confirmed.

Following weeks

Investigation reveals faulty repair of aft pressure bulkhead after 1978 tailstrike caused structural failure.
