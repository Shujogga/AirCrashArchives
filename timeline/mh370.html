Malaysia Airlines Flight 370 – Detailed Timeline

🛫 Departure & Initial Contact – March 8, 2014
00:42 MYT – March 8, 2014

Flight MH370 departs Kuala Lumpur International Airport for Beijing aboard Boeing 777-200ER (9M-MRO) with 239 people onboard.

01:07 MYT – March 8, 2014

Last ACARS transmission received. Satellite link lost shortly after.

01:19 MYT – March 8, 2014

Final voice contact: “Good night Malaysian Three Seven Zero.” Transponder stops transmitting two minutes later.

✈️ Radar Tracking & Disappearance – March 8, 2014
01:30–02:22 MYT – March 8, 2014

Military radar tracks aircraft turning west, crossing Malay Peninsula, and heading northwest over the Strait of Malacca. Last radar contact at 02:22 MYT over the Andaman Sea.

02:25–08:19 MYT – March 8, 2014

Satellite data unit responds to hourly pings. Final “log-on request” sent at 08:19 MYT, likely during fuel exhaustion.

💥 Search Efforts & Debris – March 2014–2018
March 18–April 28, 2014

Surface search in southern Indian Ocean led by Australia covers 4.5 million km². No wreckage found.

July 29, 2015

Flaperon from MH370 discovered on Réunion Island. Drift analysis confirms likely crash site in southern Indian Ocean.

January 2017

Search suspended after 120,000 km² scanned. No main wreckage located.

July 2018

Final Malaysian report concludes flight path resulted from manual inputs. Cause remains undetermined.
