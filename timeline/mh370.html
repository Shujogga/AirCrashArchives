MH370 – Southern Indian Ocean Disappearance

Date of Incident: March 8, 2014

Location: Southern Indian Ocean (presumed)

🧭 What Happened

Malaysia Airlines Flight 370, a Boeing 777-2H6ER, disappeared en route from Kuala Lumpur to Beijing. After losing contact with air traffic control over the South China Sea, the aircraft deviated westward, crossed the Malay Peninsula, and was last tracked over the Andaman Sea. Satellite data indicated it continued flying for nearly 7 hours before crashing into the southern Indian Ocean. All 239 onboard are presumed dead.

✈️ Aircraft Details

Flight Number: MH370 / MAS370

Aircraft Type: Boeing 777-2H6ER

Registration: 9M-MRO

Departure: Kuala Lumpur International Airport (KUL)

Destination: Beijing Capital International Airport (PEK)

Fatalities: 239 (227 passengers + 12 crew)

Survivors: 0 (presumed)

🔍 Key Factors

The aircraft’s transponder was disabled shortly after the final ATC handoff. Military radar tracked it westward before it vanished. Satellite “handshakes” with Inmarsat indicated a southbound trajectory. The cause remains undetermined, with theories ranging from hypoxia to deliberate pilot action. No distress signal was sent.

📆 Timeline of Events

  • 16:41 UTC – MH370 departs Kuala Lumpur
  • 17:19 UTC – Final ATC transmission: “Good night Malaysian three seven zero”
  • 17:21 UTC – Transponder signal lost
  • 18:22 UTC – Last military radar contact over Andaman Sea
  • 18:25 UTC – Satellite link re-established
  • 00:19 UTC – Final satellite handshake; presumed crash shortly after

🎙️ Cockpit Voice & Flight Data

“Good night Malaysian three seven zero.” — Final ATC transmission

Both CVR and FDR were never recovered. Satellite data and drift modeling informed search zones. The lack of cockpit recordings has hindered definitive conclusions.

⚙️ Aircraft Systems & Failures

  • Transponder and ACARS disabled manually or failed
  • Inmarsat satellite pings used to estimate flight path
  • No ELT signal received
  • Aircraft flew for ~7 hours post-loss of contact

🛡️ Aftermath and Reforms

  • Search covered 120,000 km²; suspended in 2017
  • Ocean Infinity conducted renewed search in 2018
  • ICAO mandated enhanced aircraft tracking and CVR duration
  • Debris confirmed from MH370 found in Réunion, Mozambique, and Madagascar
  • Final Malaysian report (2018) inconclusive; pilot involvement not ruled out
