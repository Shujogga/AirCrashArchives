United Airlines Flight 232 – Detailed Timeline

🛫 Departure & Engine Failure – July 19, 1989
14:09 CDT – July 19, 1989

Flight 232 departs Denver for Chicago aboard McDonnell Douglas DC-10-10 (N1819U) with 296 people onboard. Final destination: Philadelphia.

15:16 CDT – July 19, 1989

Tail-mounted engine (#2) suffers uncontained failure at 37,000 ft. Shrapnel severs all three hydraulic systems. Aircraft loses all flight controls.

⚠️ Emergency Response & Descent – July 19, 1989
15:20 CDT – July 19, 1989

Crew declares emergency. Off-duty DC-10 instructor Dennis Fitch volunteers to assist. He uses differential thrust to steer and stabilize aircraft.

15:42 CDT – July 19, 1989

Flight engineer reports damage to rear stabilizers. Crew begins fuel dump and manually lowers landing gear. Aircraft descends in phugoid oscillations.

15:55 CDT – July 19, 1989

Flight 232 lines up with Runway 22 at Sioux Gateway Airport. Crew unable to control speed or descent rate. Approach speed exceeds 215 knots.

💥 Crash Landing & Rescue – July 19, 1989
16:00 CDT – July 19, 1989

Right wing drops. Aircraft touches down off-center, cartwheels, breaks apart, and ignites. Emergency crews respond immediately.

Evening – July 19, 1989

111 fatalities, 185 survivors. Most survivors seated forward of wings. All cockpit crew survive. CVR and FDR recovered.

⚖️ Investigation & Legacy – 1989–1992
1990–1992

NTSB finds engine fan disk failed due to manufacturing defect. Hydraulic system design lacked isolation fuses. Crew praised for CRM and heroic effort.

1992–2000

FAA mandates hydraulic fuses and improved inspection protocols. Flight 232 becomes case study in CRM training worldwide.
