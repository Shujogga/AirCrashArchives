Alaska Airlines Flight 261 – Detailed Timeline

🛫 Departure & Cruise – January 31, 2000
13:37 PST – January 31, 2000

Flight 261 departs Puerto Vallarta for Seattle via San Francisco aboard McDonnell Douglas MD-83 (N963AS) with 88 people onboard.

15:49 PST – January 31, 2000

Flight crew contacts Alaska maintenance to report jammed horizontal stabilizer. Attempts to troubleshoot fail.

16:09 PST – January 31, 2000

Stabilizer suddenly frees and moves to extreme nose-down position. Aircraft enters steep dive from 31,500 ft to 23,000 ft in 80 seconds.

⚠️ Recovery & Final Descent – January 31, 2000
16:10–16:19 PST – January 31, 2000

Pilots regain partial control. Aircraft stabilizes at 24,400 ft. Crew requests descent and diversion to Los Angeles.

16:19 PST – January 31, 2000

Jackscrew assembly fails completely. Horizontal stabilizer detaches internally. Aircraft pitches nose-down and rolls inverted.

16:22 PST – January 31, 2000

Flight 261 crashes into Pacific Ocean ~2.7 miles north of Anacapa Island. All 88 occupants perish. No survivors.

💥 Investigation & Findings – 2000–2002
February–December 2000

NTSB investigation reveals jackscrew threads stripped due to inadequate lubrication. CVR and FDR recovered.

December 2002

Final report blames Alaska Airlines’ maintenance practices and FAA oversight. Lack of fail-safe mechanism cited as contributing factor.

⚖️ Aftermath & Legacy – 2003–2008
2003–2004

87 of 88 wrongful death lawsuits settled. FAA and Alaska Airlines implement new inspection protocols. Memorials established in Seattle and Port Hueneme.

2008

Alaska Airlines retires MD-80 fleet. Maintenance reforms credited with improving safety culture.
