Tenerife Airport Disaster – Detailed Timeline

🛫 Diversion & Ground Operations – March 27, 1977
13:15 WET – March 27, 1977

A bomb explodes at Gran Canaria Airport, prompting diversion of inbound flights to Los Rodeos Airport, Tenerife.

14:00–15:00 WET – March 27, 1977

KLM Flight 4805 and Pan Am Flight 1736 land at Los Rodeos. Airport becomes congested; aircraft park on the taxiway.

16:45 WET – March 27, 1977

KLM aircraft refuels at Los Rodeos. Pan Am waits behind, unable to pass due to narrow clearance.

✈️ Taxiing & Miscommunication – March 27, 1977
17:02 WET – March 27, 1977

KLM instructed to taxi down runway and turn 180° for takeoff. Pan Am follows behind, instructed to exit via third taxiway.

17:05 WET – March 27, 1977

Dense fog rolls in. Visibility drops below 100 meters. Tower cannot see aircraft positions.

17:06:12 WET – March 27, 1977

KLM reads back clearance and says “We are now at takeoff.” Tower responds “OK... stand by for takeoff, I will call you.”

17:06:18 WET – March 27, 1977

Pan Am crew radios “We’re still taxiing down the runway.” Radio interference causes partial message loss in KLM cockpit.

💥 Collision & Aftermath – March 27, 1977
17:06:45 WET – March 27, 1977

KLM begins takeoff roll. Pan Am still on runway, attempting to locate exit.

17:06:50 WET – March 27, 1977

Pan Am crew sees KLM approaching. Attempts evasive turn. KLM rotates early, tail strikes runway.

17:06:55 WET – March 27, 1977

KLM’s landing gear and engines shear through Pan Am’s fuselage. Both aircraft erupt in flames.

17:07–17:30 WET – March 27, 1977

Rescue efforts begin. 583 fatalities confirmed. 61 survivors escape from Pan Am’s forward fuselage.
