Black Hawk 2025 – Potomac River Mid-Air Collision

Date of Incident: January 29, 2025

Location: Potomac River, Washington, D.C., USA

🧭 What Happened

A U.S. Army Sikorsky UH-60L Black Hawk helicopter (PAT25) collided mid-air with American Airlines Flight 5342, a Bombardier CRJ701ER regional jet, while both were operating near Ronald Reagan Washington National Airport. The collision occurred at low altitude over the Potomac River during final approach. All 67 people aboard both aircraft were killed.

✈️ Aircraft Details

Flight Number: PAT25 (Black Hawk) / AA5342 (CRJ)

Aircraft Types: Sikorsky UH-60L Black Hawk / Bombardier CRJ701ER

Registrations: 00-26860 (Black Hawk) / N709PS (CRJ)

Origins: Fort Belvoir (DAA) / Wichita Dwight D. Eisenhower (ICT)

Destination: Fort Belvoir (DAA) / Reagan National (DCA)

Fatalities: 3 (Black Hawk) + 64 (CRJ)

Survivors: 0

🔍 Key Factors

The Black Hawk was conducting a night vision goggle check flight. The CRJ was on final approach to Runway 33. Both aircraft were cleared by ATC but operated on separate radio frequencies. The Black Hawk crew reported visual contact with the jet but may have misidentified it. A conflict alert sounded seconds before impact, but separation was not maintained.

📆 Timeline of Events

  • 01:39 UTC – AA5342 departs Wichita
  • 01:43 UTC – CRJ cleared to land on Runway 33
  • 01:46 UTC – PAT25 advised of CRJ traffic at 1,200 ft
  • 01:47:39 UTC – Conflict alert triggered
  • 01:47:42 UTC – ATC instructs PAT25 to pass behind CRJ
  • 01:47:59 UTC – Mid-air collision occurs at ~300 ft
  • 01:48:00 UTC – Both aircraft crash into Potomac River

🎙️ Cockpit Voice & Flight Data

“PAT25 has aircraft in sight, request visual separation.” — Black Hawk crew
“Traffic, traffic.” — CRJ TCAS alert

Both CVRs were recovered. The Black Hawk crew may not have received critical ATC instructions due to a mic press. The CRJ crew attempted a last-second pitch-up maneuver. The CVRs and FDRs are under analysis by NTSB and Army investigators.

⚙️ Aircraft Systems & Failures

  • Black Hawk altimeter readings may have been inaccurate
  • ADS-B not transmitting from Black Hawk; Mode S only
  • CRJ TCAS issued alert seconds before impact
  • Separate ATC frequencies limited situational awareness

🛡️ Aftermath and Reforms

  • FAA temporarily restricted helicopter traffic near DCA
  • NTSB issued urgent safety recommendations for Route 4
  • Families called for Army transparency and liaison support
  • Congress launched investigations into FAA and Army procedures
  • Memorials held in Kansas, Virginia, and D.C.
