Korean Air Flight 801 – Detailed Timeline

🛫 Departure & Descent – August 5–6, 1997
20:53 KST – August 5, 1997

Flight 801 departs Seoul’s Kimpo International Airport for Guam aboard Boeing 747-3B5 HL7468 with 254 people onboard.

01:03 ChST – August 6, 1997

Flight contacts Guam CERAP at 41,000 ft. Cleared to land on Runway 6L. Weather deteriorating with rain and low visibility.

01:13 ChST – August 6, 1997

Descent begins. Captain briefs crew on approach. Glideslope for Runway 6L is out of service, but crew mistakenly believes it’s operational.

⚠️ Approach & Confusion – August 6, 1997
01:38 ChST – August 6, 1997

Flight joins localizer. Flaps at 10°, gear up. ATC clears for ILS approach, noting glideslope unusable. Crew does not acknowledge this.

01:40 ChST – August 6, 1997

Aircraft descends below minimum safe altitude. GPWS issues “Sink Rate” and “Minimums” alerts. Crew continues descent.

01:42:26 ChST – August 6, 1997

Aircraft crashes into Bijia Peak near Nimitz Hill, ~3.3 nm from runway threshold. Impact at 660 ft elevation. Post-crash fire erupts.

💥 Rescue & Investigation – August 6–1999
Morning – August 6, 1997

Rescue hampered by terrain and fuel pipeline. 229 fatalities, 25 survivors. CVR and FDR recovered. Guam declares emergency.

January 2000

NTSB final report cites captain’s failure to execute non-precision approach, poor CRM, and fatigue. FAA criticized for disabling MSAW system at Guam.

⚖️ Legacy & Reforms – 2000–2005
2000–2005

CRM training strengthened globally. FAA revises MSAW protocols. Korean Air overhauls pilot training and approach procedures.
