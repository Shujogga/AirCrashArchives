Korean Air 801 – Guam CFIT Disaster

Date of Incident: August 6, 1997

Location: Bijia Peak, near Nimitz Hill, Guam

🧭 What Happened

Korean Air Flight 801, a Boeing 747-3B5, crashed into terrain while on approach to Guam’s Antonio B. Won Pat International Airport. The crew descended below minimum safe altitude during a non-precision approach in poor weather, mistakenly believing the glideslope was operational. The crash killed 229 of the 254 onboard.

✈️ Aircraft Details

Flight Number: KE801 / KAL801

Aircraft Type: Boeing 747-3B5

Registration: HL7468

Departure: Seoul Gimpo International Airport (GMP)

Destination: Guam International Airport (GUM)

Fatalities: 229

Survivors: 25

🔍 Key Factors

The glideslope for Runway 6L was out of service, but the captain believed it was active. The crew used outdated charts and misjudged their descent. Poor CRM and fatigue contributed to the failure to challenge the captain’s decisions.

📆 Timeline of Events

  • 11:53 UTC – Departure from Seoul
  • 13:03 UTC – Contact with Guam ATC
  • 13:38 UTC – Cleared for ILS approach (glideslope unusable)
  • 13:42 UTC – Aircraft descends below MDA
  • 13:42:26 UTC – Impact with Bijia Peak at 660 ft

🎙️ Cockpit Voice & Flight Data

“Let’s make a missed approach.” — First Officer
“Go around.” — Flight Engineer

The CVR revealed confusion over glideslope status and lack of assertiveness from the crew. The GPWS issued multiple warnings before impact. Both recorders were recovered and analyzed by the NTSB.

⚙️ Aircraft Systems & Failures

  • ILS glideslope out of service
  • Minimum Safe Altitude Warning (MSAW) inhibited by FAA patch
  • Outdated approach charts used
  • CRM breakdown and fatigue

🛡️ Aftermath and Reforms

  • FAA and Korean authorities revised CRM and approach training
  • MSAW systems re-evaluated and reactivated
  • Korean Air overhauled pilot training and safety culture
  • Memorial erected near crash site
