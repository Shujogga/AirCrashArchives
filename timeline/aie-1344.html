Air India Express Flight 1344 – Kozhikode Crash

Date of Incident: August 7, 2020

Location: Kozhikode-Calicut International Airport, India

🧭 What Happened

Flight IX-1344 was a repatriation service from Dubai to Kozhikode under the Vande Bharat Mission. During landing in heavy rain, the Boeing 737-800 overshot the tabletop runway and plunged into a gorge. The aircraft broke into two, killing 21 people including both pilots, and injuring over 100 others.

✈️ Aircraft Details

Flight Number: IX-1344

Aircraft Type: Boeing 737-8HG (SFP)

Registration: VT-AXH

Departure: Dubai International Airport (DXB)

Destination: Kozhikode-Calicut International Airport (CCJ)

Fatalities: 21 (19 passengers + 2 pilots)

Survivors: 169

🔍 Key Factors

The aircraft attempted two landings — the first on Runway 28 was aborted due to tailwind. On the second attempt on Runway 10, the aircraft touched down 1,000 meters beyond the threshold. Despite a “Go Around” call from the First Officer, the Captain continued the approach. The aircraft failed to stop and plunged off the tabletop runway.

📆 Timeline of Events

  • 10:14 UTC – Flight departs Dubai
  • 13:42 UTC – Overflies Kozhikode; first approach aborted
  • 14:07 UTC – Cleared to land on Runway 10
  • 14:10 UTC – Touchdown near taxiway C (approx. 4,438 ft down runway)
  • 14:11 UTC – Aircraft overruns and crashes into gorge
  • 16:45 UTC – Rescue operations completed

🎙️ Cockpit Voice & Flight Data

“Go around, Captain.” — First Officer
“We’ve landed halfway down.” — Final CVR entry

The CVR revealed poor Crew Resource Management and a steep cockpit authority gradient. Despite warnings, the Captain continued the approach. The CVR and FDR were recovered and analyzed by AAIB India.

⚙️ Aircraft Systems & Failures

  • Unstabilized approach with excessive speed and altitude
  • Touchdown occurred ~1,000 m beyond threshold
  • Thrust reversers delayed due to engine power burst
  • Aircraft exited runway at 84 knots and broke apart

🛡️ Aftermath and Reforms

  • AAIB Final Report cited pilot error and systemic failures
  • DGCA mandated stricter CRM and tabletop runway protocols
  • Compensation paid to victims and survivors
  • Renewed calls for EMAS installation at critical Indian airports
