Northwest Airlines Flight 255 – Detailed Timeline

🛫 Departure & Taxi – August 16, 1987
20:34 EDT – August 16, 1987

Flight 255 departs gate at Detroit Metropolitan Airport aboard McDonnell Douglas MD-82 (N312RC) with 149 passengers and 6 crew. Destination: Phoenix, with final stop in Santa Ana.

20:42 EDT – August 16, 1987

Cleared for takeoff on Runway 3C. Crew misses taxiway turn and receives revised instructions. Weather: light rain, low visibility.

⚠️ Takeoff & Stall – August 16, 1987
20:44 EDT – August 16, 1987

Aircraft begins takeoff roll. Flaps and slats not extended. Takeoff warning system fails to activate due to lack of electrical power.

20:45 EDT – August 16, 1987

Aircraft lifts off at 170 knots but fails to climb. Stick shaker activates. Aircraft rolls left and right at ~50 ft altitude.

20:45:19 EDT – August 16, 1987

Left wing strikes light pole in rental car lot. Aircraft crashes onto Middlebelt Road, disintegrates, and ignites.

💥 Impact & Aftermath – August 16–17, 1987
Evening – August 16, 1987

Crash kills 154 onboard and 2 on the ground. One survivor: 4-year-old Cecelia Cichan. 5 ground injuries reported.

August 17, 1987

CVR and FDR recovered. NTSB begins investigation. Wreckage spread across highway, embankment, and car lots.

⚖️ Investigation & Legacy – 1988–1990
May 10, 1988

NTSB final report cites failure to use taxi checklist and extend flaps/slats. Takeoff warning system lacked power. Cause of power loss undetermined.

1989–1990

FAA mandates stricter checklist adherence and warning system reliability. Airlines revise takeoff procedures and CRM training.
