Yeti Airlines Flight 691 – Detailed Timeline

🛫 Departure & Approach – January 15, 2023
10:32 NPT – January 15, 2023

Flight 691 departs Kathmandu’s Tribhuvan International Airport for Pokhara aboard ATR 72-500 (9N-ANC) with 72 people onboard.

10:51 NPT – January 15, 2023

Aircraft descends from 6,500 ft and joins downwind for Runway 12 at Pokhara International Airport. Visual approach initiated.

10:56 NPT – January 15, 2023

Flaps 15 selected, gear lowered. PF disengages autopilot at 721 ft AGL and calls for “Flaps 30.” PM mistakenly feathers both propellers instead of extending flaps.

⚠️ Loss of Thrust & Stall – January 15, 2023
10:57:07 NPT – January 15, 2023

ATC clears aircraft to land. PF reports “no power from engines.” Power levers advanced, but propellers remain feathered. Aircraft begins final turn at 368 ft AGL.

10:57:24 NPT – January 15, 2023

Stick shaker activates. Aircraft stalls and rolls left past 90°. Crew unable to recover.

10:57:32 NPT – January 15, 2023

Aircraft crashes into Seti Gorge ~1.1 nm from runway threshold. All 72 occupants perish. No post-crash fire reported.

⚖️ Investigation & Findings – 2023
January–February 2023

CVR and FDR recovered and analyzed in Singapore. Preliminary report confirms inadvertent feathering of both propellers during approach.

December 2023

Final report attributes crash to human error: PM moved condition levers instead of flap lever, causing loss of thrust and aerodynamic stall.

📚 Contributing Factors
2023

High cockpit workload, unfamiliar airport, tight visual circuit, lack of simulator training, and poor CRM cited as contributing factors.
