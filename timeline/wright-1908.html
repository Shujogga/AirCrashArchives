Wright Flyer Crash – Detailed Timeline

🛫 Demonstration Flight Begins – September 17, 1908
17:08 EST – September 17, 1908

Orville Wright takes off from Fort Myer parade ground with Lt. Thomas Selfridge aboard.

17:12 EST – September 17, 1908

Aircraft completes four circuits at ~150 feet altitude. Crowd of 2,500 watches.

⚠️ Mechanical Failure – September 17, 1908
17:14 EST – September 17, 1908

Starboard propeller splits mid-flight, striking a bracing wire and destabilizing the rudder.

17:15 EST – September 17, 1908

Aircraft pitches nose-down. Orville attempts to shut off power and recover control.

💥 Impact & Aftermath – September 17, 1908
17:16 EST – September 17, 1908

Wright Flyer crashes from ~75 feet. Lt. Selfridge suffers a fractured skull; Orville is seriously injured.

Evening – September 17, 1908

Lt. Selfridge dies in hospital — becoming the first fatality in powered flight history.

September 18, 1908

Army investigation begins. Cause identified as propeller failure and tail brace rupture.

📋 Source: U.S. Army accident report, Wright Brothers archives, and eyewitness accounts.
