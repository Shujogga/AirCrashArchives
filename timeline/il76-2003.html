IL-76 Crash – Kerman, Iran (2003)

Date of Incident: February 19, 2003

Location: Sirch Mountains, 35 km southeast of Kerman, Iran

🧭 What Happened

An Ilyushin Il-76MD operated by the Aerospace Force of the Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps crashed into mountainous terrain during descent in poor weather. All 275 onboard were killed, making it the deadliest Il-76 crash and one of Iran’s worst aviation disasters.

✈️ Aircraft Details

Flight Number: Unspecified military mission

Aircraft Type: Ilyushin Il-76MD

Registration: 15-2280

Operator: IRGC Aerospace Force

Departure: Zahedan Airport (ZAH)

Destination: Kerman Airport (KER)

Fatalities: All 275 onboard (257 passengers + 18 crew)

Survivors: 0

🔍 Key Factors

The aircraft was descending through poor weather with strong winds and limited visibility. Investigators concluded the crash was a case of Controlled Flight Into Terrain (CFIT). The crew descended below minimum safe altitude and impacted the Sirch Mountains.

📆 Timeline of Events

  • 13:30 UTC – Departure from Zahedan
  • 17:00 UTC – Begins descent into Kerman
  • 17:24 UTC – Last radar contact; aircraft disappears
  • 17:25 UTC – Impact with terrain near Mt. Sirch
  • Next morning – Wreckage located 100 meters below summit

🎙️ Cockpit Voice & Flight Data

“Descending through cloud layer…” — Final crew transmission
“We’re losing altitude too fast.” — Last CVR entry

Both CVR and FDR were recovered. The CVR revealed confusion over terrain clearance and poor visibility. No distress call was made. The transcript suggests the crew believed they were on a safe descent path.

⚙️ Aircraft Systems & Failures

  • No TCAS or terrain warning system onboard
  • Radar coverage limited in mountainous region
  • Weather: heavy snow, fog, and strong winds
  • Descent initiated prematurely below MDA

🛡️ Aftermath and Reforms

  • Iranian government blamed U.S. sanctions for limiting aircraft maintenance
  • IRGC tightened flight planning and weather protocols
  • Calls for TCAS installation on military transports
  • Memorial held in Kerman for victims
