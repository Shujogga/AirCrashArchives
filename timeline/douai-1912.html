Douai 1912 – First Fatal Mid-Air Collision

Date of Incident: June 19, 1912

Location: La Brayelle Aerodrome, Douai, France

🧭 What Happened

In the early morning haze over Douai, two French Army biplanes collided mid-air during routine military flights. The accident claimed the lives of Captain Marcel Dubois and Lieutenant Albert Peignan, marking the first fatal mid-air collision in aviation history.

✈️ Aircraft Details

Aircraft Type: Breguet biplanes

Operator: French Air Force

Departure & Destination: Douai/La Brayelle Aerodrome

Fatalities: 2 (both pilots)

Survivors: 0

🔍 Key Factors

Dense morning mist obscured visibility. Both pilots reportedly saw the impending collision and attempted evasive maneuvers — tragically, both steered upward, locking wings mid-air. The aircraft fell entangled, and Peignan died instantly. Dubois succumbed to injuries shortly after, as his wife arrived at the aerodrome.

📆 Timeline of Events

  • 10:20 UTC – Both aircraft airborne over La Brayelle Aerodrome
  • 10:21 UTC – Collision occurs in haze; wings interlock
  • 10:22 UTC – Aircraft crash to ground
  • 10:23 UTC – Lieutenant Peignan declared dead
  • 10:50 UTC – Captain Dubois dies from injuries

🎙️ Cockpit Voice & Flight Data

“They’re climbing too!” — Witness account
“Both aircraft locked wings mid-air.” — Early press report

No CVR or FDR existed in 1912, but eyewitness accounts and military dispatches reconstructed the event. The collision prompted early discussions on airspace separation and visibility protocols.

⚙️ Aircraft Systems & Failures

  • No radio communication between aircraft
  • Limited visibility due to morning haze
  • No formal air traffic control or separation standards
  • Aircraft collided while both attempted vertical avoidance

🛡️ Aftermath and Reforms

  • French military reviewed flight coordination procedures
  • Incident cited in early aviation safety literature
  • La Brayelle Aerodrome continued operations until mid-1950s
  • Dubois and Peignan remembered as pioneers of military aviation
