Tasmania Missing Small Plane – Search Ongoing

Date of Incident: August 5, 2025

Flight Path: George Town Airport (Tasmania) to Hillston Airport (NSW) via Leongatha (Victoria); last radar near Lake Barrington

🧭 What Happened

A bright green two-seater light aircraft carrying a man in his 70s, his wife in her 60s and their dog departed George Town Airport at 12:45 AEST bound for Hillston via Leongatha. No scheduled position report or mayday call was ever made, and radar contact was lost over Bass Strait.

✈️ Aircraft Details

Type/Model: Bright green two-seater light aircraft (make/model not disclosed)

Registration: Not released

Occupants: 3 (pilot, passenger, dog)

Pilot Experience: Very experienced; aircraft owned for 3–4 months

🔍 Key Factors

  • No radio, ELT or EPIRB distress signal received
  • Mandatory pre-flight contact with AMSA was not made; under investigation whether aircraft disappeared before reporting point
  • Intermittent low cloud and haze over Bass Strait hampered visibility
  • Family raised the alarm at 17:00 AEST when the flight failed to arrive

📆 Timeline of Events

  • 12:45 AEST (Sat) – Takeoff from George Town Airport
  • 13:00 AEST – Scheduled position call due; no response
  • 13:10 AEST – Last radar return near Lake Barrington
  • 17:00 AEST – Family reports missing after failure to arrive
  • 18:00 AEST – AMSA launches large-scale search

🛰️ Search Efforts

  • AMSA coordinating Challenger jet, two fixed-wing aircraft and multiple vessels
  • Tasmania Police helicopter and local volunteer SAR teams deployed
  • Extensive search across northern Tasmania, Bass Strait and southern Victoria
  • Search continuing through night and resumed at 08:00 AEST
  • Public urged to watch coastlines, open water and report any sightings

👮 Police Statement

"We remain optimistic at this stage and we are still hopeful for a very positive outcome," said Tasmania Police Northern Commander Marco Ghedini. "As these events unfold, it becomes a concern when we haven't located an aircraft or persons or debris, but we do remain hopeful."

🎙️ Communications & Flight Data

No cockpit voice recorder fitted; only routine ATC checks were logged until radar loss. EPIRB onboard must be manually activated to send a distress beacon.

🔎 Investigation Status

Authorities are examining whether the aircraft disappeared before the mandatory reporting point and investigating the failure to notify AMSA prior to departure.
