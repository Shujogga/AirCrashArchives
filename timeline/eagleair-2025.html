Eagle Air Flight 2025 – South Sudan Crash

Date of Incident: January 29, 2025

Location: Rubkona County, Unity State, South Sudan

🧭 What Happened

A Beechcraft 1900D operated by Eagle Air on behalf of Light Air Services crashed shortly after takeoff from the Greater Pioneer Operating Company (GPOC) Unity Airstrip. The aircraft was en route to Juba International Airport when it lost altitude and impacted terrain. Of the 21 onboard, 20 were killed. The sole survivor was critically injured.

✈️ Aircraft Details

Flight Number: Eagle Air 2025

Aircraft Type: Beechcraft 1900D

Registration: 5X-RHB

Operator: Eagle Air Uganda (leased to Light Air Services)

Departure: GPOC Unity Airstrip

Destination: Juba International Airport (JUB)

Fatalities: 20

Survivors: 1 (South Sudanese engineer)

🔍 Key Factors

Witnesses reported a wing possibly detached mid-flight. The aircraft lost altitude approximately 10 minutes after takeoff. The crash site showed the aircraft inverted with debris scattered widely. The aircraft was 23 years old and had previously operated in New Zealand before being acquired by Eagle Air Uganda in 2016.

📆 Timeline of Events

  • 07:12 UTC – Aircraft departs GPOC Unity Airstrip
  • 07:22 UTC – Witnesses observe loss of altitude and wing separation
  • 07:23 UTC – Aircraft crashes inverted near Rubkona
  • 07:30 UTC – Emergency response initiated
  • 08:15 UTC – Sole survivor evacuated to hospital

🎙️ Cockpit Voice & Flight Data

“We’re losing lift!” — Final cockpit entry

The CVR and FDR were recovered and sent to the United States for analysis. Preliminary data suggests structural failure may have preceded the loss of control. The sole survivor reported hearing a loud bang before impact.

⚙️ Aircraft Systems & Failures

  • Possible wing separation during climb
  • Loss of lift and control at low altitude
  • Aircraft impacted terrain inverted
  • ELT signal delayed due to terrain obstruction

🛡️ Aftermath and Reforms

  • South Sudan Civil Aviation Authority launched investigation
  • Flight data sent to U.S. for analysis
  • Concerns raised over aging fleet and infrastructure
  • Calls for improved oversight and maintenance protocols
