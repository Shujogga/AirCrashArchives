Turkish Airlines Flight 981 – Detailed Timeline

🛫 Flight Preparation & Departure – March 3, 1974
07:57 TRT – March 3, 1974

Flight 981 departs Istanbul Yeşilköy Airport with 167 passengers and 11 crew aboard a McDonnell Douglas DC-10 (TC-JAV).

11:02 CET – March 3, 1974

Aircraft lands at Paris Orly Airport. 50 passengers disembark; 216 new passengers board due to a strike at British European Airways.

12:32 CET – March 3, 1974

Flight departs Orly Airport bound for London Heathrow. Aircraft climbs and turns northwest over Meaux.

⚠️ Cargo Door Failure & Decompression – March 3, 1974
~12:42 CET – March 3, 1974

Rear cargo door blows off at ~11,000 ft. Explosive decompression causes cabin floor collapse and severs control cables.

~12:43 CET – March 3, 1974

Six passengers are ejected from the aircraft. Pilots lose control of elevators, rudder, and engine #2.

~12:43:30 CET – March 3, 1974

Overspeed warning activates. Cockpit voice recorder captures crew shouting “The fuselage has burst!”

💥 Impact & Aftermath – March 3, 1974
~12:44:47 CET – March 3, 1974

Aircraft crashes into Ermenonville Forest near Fontaine-Chaalis, France at ~783 km/h. All 346 onboard perish.

Afternoon – March 3, 1974

Rescue teams recover over 20,000 body fragments. Only 188 bodies are identifiable.

March 4–10, 1974

Investigation begins. Cargo door design flaw and improper latching confirmed as root cause.
