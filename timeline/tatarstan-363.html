Tatarstan Airlines Flight 363 – Detailed Timeline

🛫 Departure & Approach – November 17, 2013
18:20 MSK – November 17, 2013

Flight 363 departs Moscow Domodedovo Airport for Kazan aboard Boeing 737-53A (VQ-BBN) with 50 people onboard.

19:15 MSK – November 17, 2013

Aircraft approaches Kazan Airport. Crew notes navigation map displacement but does not request vectors. Approach flown 4 km north of published procedure.

19:21 MSK – November 17, 2013

ATC clears aircraft to land on Runway 29. Crew acknowledges. Aircraft is misaligned and unstable on final approach.

⚠️ Go-Around & Loss of Control – November 17, 2013
19:22 MSK – November 17, 2013

First Officer initiates go-around due to non-landing position. TO/GA mode selected; autopilot disengages. Flaps retracted to 15°, gear remains down.

19:22:30 MSK – November 17, 2013

Aircraft climbs to 700 m (2,300 ft). Pitch angle reaches 25°. Airspeed drops to 117 knots. Captain initiates nose-down input without trimming.

19:23 MSK – November 17, 2013

Aircraft enters steep dive. Pitch angle reaches −75°. EGPWS issues “Sink Rate” and “Pull Up” warnings. No corrective action taken.

💥 Impact & Aftermath – November 17–18, 2013
19:23:28 MSK – November 17, 2013

Aircraft crashes between runway and taxiways at Kazan Airport. Impact speed exceeds 450 km/h. All 50 occupants perish. Post-crash fire erupts.

November 18, 2013

Airport closed for 24 hours. IAC investigators arrive. CVR and FDR recovered. Criminal investigation launched.

⚖️ Investigation & Findings – 2013–2015
December 2015

IAC final report concludes crash caused by pilot error during go-around. Captain lacked upset recovery skills. Airline oversight and training found deficient.

November 2019

Russian Investigative Committee confirms falsified pilot documents and systemic regulatory failures. Safety management system deemed non-functional.
