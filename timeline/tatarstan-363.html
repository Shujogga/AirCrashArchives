Tatarstan 363 – Kazan Go-Around Dive

Date of Incident: November 17, 2013

Location: Kazan International Airport, Russia

🧭 What Happened

Tatarstan Airlines Flight 363, a Boeing 737-500, crashed during a go-around attempt at Kazan Airport. The crew initiated the maneuver due to an unstable approach but failed to control the aircraft, which entered a steep dive and impacted the ground at a 75° nose-down angle. All 50 onboard were killed. The crash was attributed to spatial disorientation and lack of upset recovery skills.

✈️ Aircraft Details

Flight Number: U9363 / TAK363

Aircraft Type: Boeing 737-53A

Registration: VQ-BBN

Departure: Moscow Domodedovo Airport (DME)

Destination: Kazan International Airport (KZN)

Fatalities: All 50 onboard

Survivors: 0

🔍 Key Factors

The crew failed to recognize autopilot disengagement during the go-around. The captain, who had limited experience and falsified training records, pitched the aircraft nose-down after a steep climb. The aircraft entered a dive with increasing airspeed and negative pitch, culminating in a high-speed impact. A “map shift” error in navigation contributed to the unstable approach.

📆 Timeline of Events

  • 14:25 UTC – Departure from Moscow
  • 15:21 UTC – Cleared to land on Runway 29
  • 15:22 UTC – Go-around initiated due to unstable approach
  • 15:23 UTC – Aircraft climbs to 700 m, then pitches nose-down
  • 15:23:28 UTC – Impact between runway and taxiway

🎙️ Cockpit Voice & Flight Data

“Going around, non-landing position.” — First Officer

The CVR captured confusion and lack of coordination during the go-around. The FDR showed no control inputs for 25 seconds after TOGA activation. The aircraft pitched up to 25°, then rapidly down to −75°. Both recorders were recovered and analyzed by MAK and international teams.

⚙️ Aircraft Systems & Failures

  • Autopilot disengaged during TOGA; crew failed to notice
  • Flaps retracted from 30° to 15°, increasing pitch-up moment
  • No manual control inputs for 25 seconds
  • Spatial disorientation led to excessive pitch-down input

🛡️ Aftermath and Reforms

  • MAK final report released December 2015
  • Captain’s training records found falsified
  • Tatarstan Airlines shut down in 2014
  • Russian authorities revised oversight of pilot licensing
  • ICAO emphasized upset recovery and go-around training
