Delta CRJ-900 – Toronto Crash

Date of Incident: February 17, 2025

Location: Toronto Pearson International Airport, Canada

🧭 What Happened

Delta Connection Flight 4819, operated by Endeavor Air, was a scheduled service from Minneapolis to Toronto. During landing in gusty conditions, the Bombardier CRJ-900LR experienced a hard touchdown that fractured the right main landing gear. The aircraft flipped over and caught fire. All 80 onboard survived, though 21 were injured.

✈️ Aircraft Details

Flight Number: DL4819 / EDV4819

Destination: Toronto Pearson International (YYZ)

Fatalities: 0

Injuries: 21

🔍 Key Factors

Wind gusts caused a rapid descent just before touchdown. The aircraft landed with a 7.5° right bank and a descent rate of 1,098 fpm — exceeding gear design limits. The right gear collapsed, the wing detached, and fuel ignited. The aircraft came to rest inverted.

🎙️ Cockpit Voice & Flight Data

“Sink rate.” — EGPWS alert
“We’re in a dive here.” — Captain

The CVR captured a rapid descent and thrust reduction. The aircraft touched down with excessive vertical acceleration. Both CVR and FDR were recovered and analyzed by the TSB.

⚙️ Aircraft Systems & Failures

  • Landing gear fractured due to excessive descent rate
  • Right wing detached, releasing fuel
  • Fire erupted on impact; fuselage inverted
  • Cockpit door jammed; crew exited via ceiling hatch

🛡️ Aftermath and Reforms

  • TSB launched full investigation (A25O0021)
  • Delta offered $30,000 compensation per passenger
  • Runway closed for 3 hours; flights diverted
  • Focus on hard landing training and gear certification
