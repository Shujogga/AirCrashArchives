Date: March 21, 2022
Aircraft: Boeing 737-89P (B-1791)
Operator: China Eastern Airlines
Fatalities: 132 (all onboard)
Cause: Under investigation; aircraft entered near-vertical dive from cruise altitude
No official cockpit voice recorder transcript has been released for this accident. The aircraft was cruising at 29,100 feet when it suddenly entered a near-vertical dive, descending at over 31,000 feet per minute. It briefly recovered altitude before diving again and impacting terrain near Wuzhou, Guangxi. Both the CVR and FDR were recovered and analyzed by Chinese authorities, with assistance from the U.S. National Transportation Safety Board (NTSB) and Boeing. As of now, no mechanical faults have been publicly confirmed, and speculation remains about possible intentional input or control surface anomalies. Due to the sensitive nature of the investigation, CVR contents have not been disclosed. The final report is still pending, and no transcript excerpts have been made available to the public.
