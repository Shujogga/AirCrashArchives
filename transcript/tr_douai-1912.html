Date: June 19, 1912
Aircraft: Breguet biplanes
Operator: French Army
Fatalities: 2 (Capt. Marcel Dubois & Lt. Albert Peignan)
Cause: Mid-air collision in early morning haze during maneuvers
No cockpit voice recorder or flight data recorder existed in 1912. The accident was reconstructed from eyewitness accounts and press reports. During a military training flight at Douai/La Brayelle Aerodrome, Capt. Dubois and Lt. Peignan were flying separate Breguet biplanes when they collided at approximately 75 feet altitude. The early morning haze obscured visibility, and both aircraft struck each other while maneuvering. The collision caused the wings and wires to entangle, sending both planes crashing to the ground. Lt. Peignan died instantly; Capt. Dubois succumbed to injuries shortly after.
