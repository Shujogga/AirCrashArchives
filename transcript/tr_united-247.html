United Airlines Flight 247 – Transcript

Date: October 20, 1948

Aircraft: Douglas DC-6

Operator: United Airlines

Fatalities: 43 (all onboard)

Cause: In-flight fire and loss of control; suspected electrical fault

No cockpit voice recorder or flight data recorder was installed on Flight 247, as the accident occurred before such devices were mandated. The investigation relied on witness accounts, wreckage analysis, and radio communications. The aircraft reported smoke in the cabin and requested an emergency descent before contact was lost. The fire spread rapidly, and the plane crashed near Mount Carmel, Pennsylvania.
